ABOUT THE PROGRAM
Freshman Retreat is a weekend residential program for first- and second-year Ismaili college students. The program aims to bring students from a single region together at a campsite for a weekend of networking, outdoor activities, and fun! Students can participate in activities such as ziplining, archery, and swimming, depending on the site. Students also learn about navigating internships and mental health, while also toasting s’mores and enjoying nature. Implemented originally in 2022, Freshman Retreat is a program of the Aga Khan Youth and Sports Board (AKYSB) for the United States.

FALL 2024
Fall Freshman Retreat will take place from Friday, October 11 - Sunday, October 13 at Rock Eagle 4-H Center. Students should plan on arriving Friday evening around 5PM and departing Sunday afternoon after 2PM.
​
Freshmen and sophomores in undergraduate programs in the Southeast, Florida, Northeast, Midwest, and West are eligible to attend the program. Students that have previously attended a Freshman Retreat are not eligible for this fall’s program.

Transportation will be provided from metro Atlanta on Friday afternoon to the site. Transportation will also be available on Sunday afternoon to return back to metro Atlanta. Students arriving from other regions by flight will be provided airport rides. Students flying into Atlanta should aim to land no later than 2:00PM on Friday in order to take advantage of rides from the airport, and to take the bus with other metro Atlanta students to the site later in the afternoon. If you have trouble booking flights or have questions around transportation, please email us at team@ysbfreshmanretreat.org.

Rock Eagle 4-H is 90 minutes from Atlanta and 60 minutes from Athens. More information on the site can be found here.

Purpose + Content
The purpose of the retreat is to enable Ismaili college students to get to know other Ismaili college students from their own campus as well as other campuses in the region, so much of the weekend is designed to allow participants to get to know each other. The program schedule includes plenty of unstructured time during which participants can take advantage of the indoor and outdoor activities at the site or simply just hang out or relax. These outdoor activities include ropes courses, astronomy, archery, and more. Based on input from college students we will also be incorporating some sessions that allow incoming students to meet upperclassmen at their colleges who can provide tips on how to navigate campus and and other sessions through which program participants can learn about summer internships and career planning, mental health, navigating their faith, and resources for students on campus and through the Jamati Institutions.
